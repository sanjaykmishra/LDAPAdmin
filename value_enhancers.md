Highest signal — things buyers are actively complaining don't exist:
Role-based access drift detection. The single most common audit finding we saw across every source is "privilege creep" — users accumulating permissions over time as roles change. No mid-market tool proactively surfaces this. A simple "this user's access is anomalous compared to their peer group" flag, even without ML, would be a genuine first in your price tier. The SOX runbook sources specifically call out that auditors look for this and most orgs have no answer.
Termination velocity reporting. BambooHR fires a termination event — how long until LDAP access is actually revoked? Right now orgs have no visibility into this gap. A dashboard showing average and worst-case time-to-revoke per department would be the kind of thing a CISO screenshots for their board. It also makes your BambooHR integration feel like a strategic capability rather than a connector.
Auditor portal. A read-only, time-scoped view you can share directly with an external auditor — no login required beyond a signed link, with a clear expiry. Right now orgs email ZIP files and then field questions. An auditor portal that lets them browse the evidence package, verify the HMAC signature, and export what they need would eliminate that back-and-forth entirely. No competitor has this at your price point.
Orphaned account detection. Users who left the company but still have active directory entries. This appears in nearly every compliance checklist we found and is universally described as something orgs discover embarrassingly late. Auto-surfacing orphans by cross-referencing BambooHR status against LDAP would be a one-click win during an audit.
Medium signal — table stakes as you move upmarket:
Service account governance. Every source mentioned that service accounts "slip through the cracks" — they're not tied to a person, so they never get reviewed. A separate review workflow for service accounts with an ownership assignment model would address this. ConductorOne mentions it, nobody below $20k implements it well.
Review fatigue mitigation. The "rubber stamping" problem — managers clicking approve on everything without reading it — is cited by SailPoint, Zluri, and the SOX runbook sources. Risk-scoring individual access items (flag unusual permissions, inactive accounts, cross-department access) so reviewers see what actually needs attention rather than 200 identical rows. Even a simple heuristic version of this is differentiated.
Policy templates by framework. Pre-built review configurations for SOC 2 Type II, SOX Section 404, and ISO 27001 that set the right frequencies, scopes, and evidence formats automatically. Reduces the "where do I even start" friction for a first-time compliance hire enormously.
Lower priority but worth noting:
Slack/Teams notifications for review campaigns rather than email. Every vendor mentions email fatigue in review campaigns. Reviewers respond faster to Slack pings, and it signals modern tooling to tech-company buyers.
Read-only Entra and Google Workspace connectors before full write support. Even just pulling identity data from those sources for review purposes would accelerate the roadmap value without requiring full bidirectional integration.
The one I'd prioritize above everything else:
Termination velocity reporting combined with the auditor portal. Together they tell a story that no other product at your price tells: "we don't just generate evidence, we prove the controls are working in real time and we make it trivially easy for your auditor to verify." That's the sentence that closes deals.
